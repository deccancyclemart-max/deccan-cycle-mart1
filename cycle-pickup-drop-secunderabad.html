üí° Your At-Home Bike Maintenance Checklist

Between professional services, a little care at home goes a long way. Performing this simple check every month will help extend your bike‚Äôs life and performance:

The A-B-C Quick Check

  • Air: Properly inflated tires are your first line of defense against punctures. Check the side of your tire for the recommended PSI (Pounds per Square Inch) and top up the air weekly.
  • Brakes: Before every ride, squeeze both brake levers. They should feel firm, not spongy, and should stop the wheel effectively without rubbing when released.
  • Chain: Look at your chain. Is it dry, dirty, or rusty? A clean, lubricated chain is a happy chain. Wipe it with a rag and apply a few drops of proper bicycle chain lube while turning the pedals backward.

The Monthly Once-Over

  • Check for Looseness: Gently try to wiggle your wheels, handlebars, and seat. Nothing should feel loose or wobbly. Pay special attention to the bolts around the handlebars and seat post.
  • Listen for Noises: A well-maintained bike is nearly silent. If you hear any clicking, grinding, or squeaking, it's a sign that something needs attention.
